The Taj Mahal is a world-famous monument located in Agra, India. 
It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631. 
Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed around 1653. 
The building is made of white marble and is known for its beautiful design and symmetry. 
It is a symbol of love and is considered one of the most beautiful buildings in the world. 
The architect of the Taj Mahal was Ustad Ahmad Lahauri. The complex includes a main dome, a mosque, a guest house, and lovely gardens. 
Over the years, it has attracted millions of visitors from around the world. In 1983, it was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site. 
Today, the Taj Mahal stands as a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and a lasting symbol of love and devotion.
