The Brihadeeswarar Temple, also known as Peruvudaiyar Kovil, is a grand Hindu temple located in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, India. 
It was built by the great Chola emperor Raja Raja Chola I in the 11th century and completed in 1010 AD. 
Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple is one of the largest and most important examples of Dravidian architecture. 
Made entirely of granite, the temple features a massive tower (called vimana) that rises about 66 meters, making it one of the tallest temple towers in the world. 
The main dome is topped with a single granite stone weighing around 80 tons. 
The temple is known for its detailed sculptures, frescoes, and inscriptions that provide insights into the Chola dynasty's history and culture. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and part of the "Great Living Chola Temples."
The Brihadeeswarar Temple is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of Tamil art, architecture, and engineering brilliance.
